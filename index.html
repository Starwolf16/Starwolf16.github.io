Come Join the 2nd Great Awakening

The Second Great Awakening is when the Protestant Religion is going through a revial. We are led by Baptist and Methodist leaders, for the most part, and is a reaction to skepticism, deism, and Rational Chrisitanity. The In the Second Great awakening, we believe in Arminian Theology, which says that every person can be saved through revivals, repentance, and conversion.

Our Leaders

Charles Grandison Finney Lyman Beecher Barton Stone Peter Cartwright
Charles Gandison Finney converted to Christianity in 1821, and since then, he has been one of our most prominent speakers in the Northern States. He is constantly filling the largest speech halls available, and conducting powerful revival meetings all over the eastern cities. His revival meeting in Rochester, New York caused 1500 more revivals in towns and villages. Lyman Beecher is one of the preachers of our movement. He started off preaching at a Presbyterian Church, but eventually moved to a Calvinistic Church. When he was called to the Hanover Church of Boston, he started preaching against unitarianism, which was sweeping the area. He went on to teach at the Lane Seminary in Concinnati. There, he taught many ministers to win the West for Protestantism. Barton W. Stone is an important Minister in the Second Great Awakening, and he helped to lead the cane Ridge Revival, which was a several day communion season attened by 20,000 people. When he disagreed with the Kentucky synod, he and 4 other ministers created the Springfield Presbytry, which attracted 15 congregations in Ohio and Kentucky. Peter Cartwright is a Methodist Revivalist who helped start the Second Great Awakening. He has personally baptized 12,000 converts. Since he is opposed to slavery, he moved from Kentucky to Illinois, and was elected to the lower house of the Illinois Great Assembly in 1828 and 1832. When he was a Methodist Circuit rider, he rode the circuits in Kentucky and Illinois, as well as Tennessee, Indiana, and Ohio.
